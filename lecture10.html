Volume of Solid of Revolution

If a region in a plane is revolved around a line in that plane, the resulting solid is called a solid of revolution.

Notice

When we revolve an entire region bounded by a curve \(y=f(x)\) around the \(x\)-axis:

  • Cross sections are circles: \(A(x)=\pi r^2\).

  • \(r = f(x)\): the radius of each circle is \(f(x)\)!

  • \(V =\displaystyle \int_a^b \pi (f(x))^2 dx\)

Example

Find the volume of the solid formed by revolving the region bounded above by \(y=x^2\), below by the \(x\)-axis, between \(x=0\) and \(x=2\), around the \(x\)-axis.

Solution

\[\begin{aligned} V &= \int_0^2 \pi (x^2)^2 dx \\ &= \pi \int_0^2 x^4 dx \\ &= \pi \left.\frac{x^5}{5} \right|_0^2 \\ &= \frac{32\pi}{5} \approx 20.1 \end{aligned}\]

Washer Method

Cross Sections

  • Cross sections are washers, outer radius\(R\), inner radius \(r\)

  • Area of a washer: \(\pi (R^2 - r^2)\)

  • \(R = f(x)\), \(r=g(x)\)

  • \(V = \int_a^b \pi [(f(x))^2 - (g(x))^2] dx\)

Example

Find the volume of the solid formed by revolving the region bounded by \(y=sin(x)\) and \(y=cos(x)\) from \(x=0\) to \(x=/frac{/pi}{4}\) around the \(x\)-axis.

Solution

\[\begin{aligned} V &= \int_0^{\pi/4} \pi (\cos^2(x) - \sin^2(x)) dx \\ &= \pi \int_0^{\pi/4} \cos(2x) dx \\ &= \left. \pi \frac{\sin(2x)}{2} \right|_0^{\pi/4} \\ &= \pi \cdot \frac{\sin(\pi/2)}{2} = \frac{\pi}{2}\end{aligned}\]
