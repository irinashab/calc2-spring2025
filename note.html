Notes to Students

My experience in teaching calculus tells me that the greatest obstacle to learning calculus is not the new ideas of calculus. These are often easily understood. Rather students find a greater struggle with the prerequisite skills - most notably algebra and trigonometry. No one knows your weaknesses better than you do, so you should put in the work where it is needed. Your progress with calculus will be far less difficult if you have a solid understanding of algebra and trig. I can run a review session upon request.
